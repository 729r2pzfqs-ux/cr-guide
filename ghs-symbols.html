GHS Hazard Symbols Guide

The Globally Harmonized System (GHS) uses 9 standard pictograms to communicate chemical hazards worldwide. These red diamond symbols are required on labels under the EU CLP Regulation.

GHS01 — Explosive

Unstable explosives, self-reactive substances, organic peroxides that may explode.

H200-H205, H240-H241

GHS02 — Flammable

Flammable gases, liquids, solids, aerosols. Self-heating or pyrophoric substances.

H220-H228, H250-H252

GHS03 — Oxidizing

May cause or intensify fire. Oxidizing gases, liquids, and solids.

H270-H272

GHS04 — Gas Under Pressure

Compressed, liquefied, dissolved, or refrigerated gases. May explode if heated.

H280-H281

GHS05 — Corrosive

Causes severe skin burns and eye damage. May corrode metals.

H290, H314, H318

GHS06 — Acute Toxicity

Fatal or toxic if swallowed, inhaled, or in contact with skin.

H300-H311, H330-H331

GHS07 — Harmful / Irritant

Harmful if swallowed/inhaled. Causes skin/eye irritation. May cause allergic reaction.

H302, H312, H315, H317, H319, H332, H335-H336

GHS08 — Health Hazard

Carcinogen, mutagen, reproductive toxin. May damage organs or cause respiratory sensitization.

H304, H334, H340-H373

GHS09 — Environmental

Very toxic to aquatic life with long lasting effects. Avoid release to the environment.

H400-H411
